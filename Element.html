Understanding the Parts of a CMS Website

1. Header

The header is the topmost part of a website. It typically contains:

  • The website's logo
  • The navigation menu
  • A search bar or social links

2. Content Area

This is the central portion of a website that displays:

  • Text (e.g. articles, descriptions)
  • Images (photos, icons, illustrations)
  • Videos (YouTube embeds, tutorials)
  • Audio (podcasts, background music)
Example: Blog posts, product descriptions, contact forms.

3. Footer

This is the section located at the bottom of a website. It often includes:

  • Copyright notice
  • Privacy policy or terms of service
  • Links to other pages or social media icons

4. Pages

A website is made up of multiple pages. Common examples include:

  • Home – Main landing page
  • About – Company info or history
  • Contact – Contact form and location
  • Services – What the business offers
Pages are the building blocks of any website.

5. Navigation Menu

This helps users move between different parts of the website. It can be:

  • Horizontal – Across the top of the page
  • Vertical – Down the left or right side
  • Dropdown / Hamburger Menu – Especially for mobile view

6. CTA (Call-To-Action)

CTAs are buttons or links that prompt users to take a specific action. Examples include:

Login Submit Buy Now Contact Us Learn More Get Started Add to Cart Sign Up

Bonus: Other Elements That Make Up a Website

  • Links – <a href="...">Click Here</a>
  • Images – <img src="..." alt="...">
  • Tables – To organize data in rows and columns
  • Forms – Used to collect input from users (e.g. login, contact)
  • Iframe – To embed external websites (e.g. YouTube, Bing, Google)
